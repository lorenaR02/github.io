Pain Relief

What if my period is painful? What do I do?

Getting your period can be painful due to cramps,lower back pains, headaches, migraines and other symptoms. To make menstruation less painful or pain free, try some of these methods:

  • PainKillers: Take ibuprofin(advil) or acetaminophen(tylenol). Take the medicine before your period starts to avoid the pain. If this doesn't work talk to your doctor to get something stronger.
  • Heat: Put heating pad, warm cloth or water bottle on your belly to sooth your belly.
  • Shower: Take a warm shower or bath
  • Use pads instead of tampons
  • Exercise: Go on walks or do light exercises
  • Massages: Rub your belly or back to relieve some of the pain
  • Stay Hydrated: Avoid alcohol and drink lots of water
  • Diet: Eat healthy foods and avoid salt and caffeine.
  • Hormonal birth control: Talk to your doctor about taking pill.
  • Naps:Taking a 10-20 minute nap before 4pm can be refreshing and energizing (Don't forget to sleep 8 hrs of sleep).

If nothing is helping you feel better talk, to a doctor.
