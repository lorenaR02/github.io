Menstruation

What is a Period?

Every month, tissue and blood buildup in the uterus to prepare for a fertilized egg. If the egg is not fertilized, the lining leaves the body through the vagina. Menstrual cycles last between 21 and 35 days but this varies for each persons body.

Signs and symptoms that you might get your period soon!

  • Acne
  • Breasts are tender or swollen
  • Fatigue
  • Cramps
  • Have diarrhea or constipation
  • Being bloated and gassy
  • Headaches or migraines
  • Mood swings
  • Anxiety and depression
  • Lower back pain

    • See a Doctor If:

  • You have blood clots in your menstrual flow that are larger than a quarter.
  • Your pain happens at times other than just before your period or during your period
  • You get irregular periods after having normal cycles.
  • Your period happens more often than every 24 days or less often than every 38 days.
  • Your period lasts longer than 8 days.
  • You bleed through one or more pads or tampons every one to two hours.
  • You feel dizzy, lightheaded, weak, or tired, or if you have chest pain or trouble breathing during or after your period.
  • You have gone three months without a period and are not pregnant or breastfeeding.
  • You have not gotten your period by age 15 or not started your period within 3 years after breast growth began, or if breasts haven’t started to grow by age 13.
  • Your headaches change.
  • You have side effects from your medicine for menstruation miranes
  • You take hormonal birth control pills and have migraine with aura (seeing bright flashing lights or spots that aren’t real).
  • Your headaches are worse when you lie down
