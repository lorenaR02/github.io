Emergency Contraception

Emergency Contraception is used when the original form of contraception fails or was not used... Mistakes happen and it is ok.

There are two main forms of contraception:

Morning after pill:

There are two types of the morning after pill. Ella and Plan B. Ella requires perscription and can be used to prevent pregnancy for up to 5 days after unprotected sex. Plan B works the same way as ella but does not require prescription, however it is not as effective as ella after 3 days. Both ella and Plan B work by changing the hormone balance in your body by adding progestin. They do one of three things: temporarily stop the release of an egg from the ovary, prevent fertilization, or prevent a fertilized egg from attaching to the uterus.

Copper IUD:

 The copper IUD is the most effective form of emergency contraception. It can be inserted up to 5 days after unprotected sex and will stop the unwanted pregnancy. The copper IUD can also continue to protect from unwanted pregnancy for up to twelve years after it is inserted. The copper IUD require a procedure to insert and often require an appointment making them sometimes less accesible.
