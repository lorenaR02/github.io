STDs

STDs or STIs are infections that are passed from one person to another during vaginal, anal, and oral sex. They’re really common, and lots of people who have them don’t have any symptoms. STDs can be dangerous, but the good news is that getting tested is no big deal, and most STDs are easy to treat.

What and How?

STDs vs STIs: STD stands for sexually transmitted disease and STI stands for sexually transmitted infection, STI is more accurate however they mean the same thing.

STD's are caused by bacteria, parasites, yeast, and viruses. Antibiotics can usually cure STD's caused by bacteria, parasites, yeast, and viruses. There is no cure for STD's caused by viruses but there are medications used to reduce symptom and control the disease.

STDs effects both men and women, however they tend to have more severe health problems in women and if the women is pregnant it can cause health poblems for the baby.

Symptoms: Even though some STD's do not have symptoms, making them asymptomatic, many do. Possible symptoms include: painful urination, lower abdominal pain, unusual discharge from both male and female genitals, and many others. Women can also have pain during sexual intercourse and vaginal bleeding between periods. Men can also have testicular pain.

Examples

Chlamydia: A bacterial infection that's easily cured with antibiotic medicine. It's one of the most common STDs, and most people who have chlamydia don't show any symptoms.

Genital Warts: Genital warts are common and are caused by certain types of HPV. Genital warts can be annoying, but they're treatable and aren't dangerous.

Gonorrhea: Gonorrhea is a common bacterial infection that's easily cured with antibiotic medicine. It's sexually transmitted, and most people with gonorrhea don't have symptoms.

Herpes: Herpes is a common virus that causes sores on your genitals and/or mouth. Herpes can be annoying and painful, but it usually doesn’t lead to serious health problems.

HIV & AIDS: HIV is the virus that causes AIDS. It damages your immune system, making it easier for you to get sick. HIV is spread during sex, but condoms can help protect you.

HPV: HPV stands for human papillomavirus. It’s the most common sexually transmitted infection. HPV is usually harmless and goes away by itself, but some types can lead to cancer or genital warts.

Prevention and Testing

In order to prevent getting STDs it is important to practice safer sex (also known as safe sex). Safer sex is all about protecting yourself and your partners from sexually transmitted infections.

Using Barriers: There are several methods possible for practicing safer sex. The first method is putting a physical barrier between you and your partner during sex. This can be in the form of condoms, internal condoms, and dental damns. These barriers prevent you and your parnter from body fluids and some skin to skin contact that could potentially spread STDs.

Getting Tested: Since many STDs do not have symptoms testing is the only way to be sure that you and/or your partner do not have an STD. Getting tested regulalrly is a very important part of safer sex.

Refrianing from taking substances like drugs and alcohol is also a way of practicing safer sex. When under the influence, it is easy to lose sight of the importance of practicing safer sex, resulting in possibly harmful decisions.
